This project aims to integrate SDG #13: Climate Action into a high school science unit on Weather Systems, providing students with the knowledge and tools to understand and address climate change.

Objectives

  • Understand the Sustainable Development Goals, focusing on SDG #13.
  • Learn the science behind climate change and its effects on weather systems.
  • Use real-time data and AI tools to analyze climate change.
  • Propose actionable strategies for combating climate change in local communities.

Lesson Plan Breakdown

Day 1: Introduction to SDGs and Climate Change

Objective: Introduce the UN’s 2030 Sustainable Development Goals, focusing on SDG #13.

  • Activities:
    • Lecture: Present an overview of the 17 SDGs with a specific focus on SDG #13: Climate Action.
    • Discussion: Facilitate a class discussion on the current climate crisis, including global warming, greenhouse gas emissions, and their effects on Earth’s ecosystems and weather systems.
    • Video: Show a short documentary clip or animation (e.g., from the UN or NASA) that explains how climate change affects daily weather patterns, biodiversity, and human livelihoods.
  • Homework: Students research the impact of climate change on their local community. They will document changes in temperature, rainfall, or extreme weather events over the past 10-20 years.

Day 2: Understanding Weather Systems and Climate Change

Objective: Learn the basics of weather systems and how climate change is altering them.

  • Activities:
    • Lecture: Teach about weather phenomena such as air masses, fronts, high and low-pressure systems, and the hydrological cycle. Relate these concepts to climate change.
    • Case Study: Analyze extreme weather events (e.g., hurricanes, droughts, heatwaves) and link their increasing frequency and severity to climate change.
    • Group Discussion: Students discuss how their research findings from Day 1 show signs of changing weather patterns in their community.
  • Homework: Students will read an article or watch a video on the relationship between climate change and extreme weather events (e.g., hurricanes, wildfires, floods) and prepare to discuss how these relate to the scientific concepts learned in class.

Day 3: Hands-on Activity: Analyzing Real-time Climate Data

Objective: Introduce students to satellite data and other real-time weather information to track climate change.

  • Activities:
    • Introduction to Space Agency Data: Provide an overview of tools such as NASA Earth Science Data, ESA’s Copernicus program, and NOAA’s climate datasets. Demonstrate how to access and interpret satellite imagery and temperature or CO₂ datasets.
    • Hands-On Data Activity: Students will work in groups to analyze real-time weather and climate data from space agencies, tracking changes in temperature, carbon dioxide levels, sea surface temperatures, and ice cover using platforms like NASA’s EarthData, NOAA’s Climate Dashboard, and ESA’s Climate Change Initiative.
    • Data Visualization: Groups will create visualizations (graphs, maps) using the data to show trends in their assigned climate variable over the last 50 years.
  • Homework: Students will prepare a one-page reflection on the insights they gained from the data and how it connects to local or global climate trends.

Day 4: Use of Artificial Intelligence in Climate Predictions

Objective: Teach students how AI tools can be used to model and predict future climate trends and weather patterns.

  • Activities:
    • Introduction to AI Tools: Present AI platforms like Google Earth Engine and Climate AI that are used for climate modeling.
    • Group Activity: Students use Google Earth Engine to analyze how land use and forest cover changes have impacted local climate conditions.
    • AI Predictions: Use Climate AI to generate predictions for how climate change might affect their region over the next 50 years, focusing on extreme weather events, temperature anomalies, and shifts in precipitation patterns.
  • Homework: Each group creates a report that discusses their AI-based findings, focusing on how future climate change could affect their local community or country.

Day 5: Climate Action Proposals and Presentations

Objective: Enable students to apply their knowledge by creating actionable climate action proposals.

  • Activities:
    • Group Work: Students will develop climate action proposals addressing a specific climate challenge (e.g., increasing heatwaves, coastal flooding, or forest degradation).
    • Presentations: Each group presents their findings and proposals in class.
  • Assessment: Students will be graded on the clarity of their analysis, creativity of their proposed solutions, and their ability to connect their proposal to the SDGs.
